About

Gabriel Kafka is a structural engineer building practical tools, interfaces, and small systems that make technical work faster to understand and easier to execute.

This page is the personal layer. The structural sandbox stays focused on calculators, plots, and engineering experiments. Here the emphasis is broader: process, taste, and the kind of work worth making.

Current Work

I work across structural engineering, computational workflows, and stripped-down web interfaces. Most of the projects are direct responses to friction: repeated calculations, poor software ergonomics, or information that should be clearer than it is.

That usually means one of three things: engineering utilities, interactive reference tools, or compact web pages that explain a model without unnecessary ceremony.

Area Focus
STRUCTURES Concrete, steel, timber, and the day-to-day logic of design decisions.
SOFTWARE Small applications and calculators that turn tedious engineering tasks into repeatable workflows.
INTERFACES Monospace-first pages, terminal-inspired layouts, and dense presentations that privilege clarity over ornament.

Working Style

I prefer tools that are legible, inspectable, and fast. The best software in technical fields should reduce ambiguity, reveal assumptions, and stay out of the way once it is doing its job.

If a workflow can be made simpler without making it dumber, that is usually the right move.
